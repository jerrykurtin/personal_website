Howdy! I'm Jerry, a computer science student at Texas A&M. I have a passion for working as a team to bring out people's unique talents, and I love constructive discomfort - putting myself into situations that require leaving my comfort zone and learning new skills. My coding journey started when I created my high school's first coding club, a group that met weekly and learned to code through websites like Codecademy. After high school, I worked at a navy research contractor called Applied Research Laboratories, where I used computer vision to classify whale calls and studied computer vision's vulnerability to malicious manipulation, attacking my models and rebuilding them to be less vulnerable to attacks.

In college, I've done research at the Texas A&M DIVE Lab. In January 2023, I was a co-author of the paper “Learning Protein Representations via Complete 3D Graph Networks,” which was accepted into ICLR, a premier machine learning conference. In part of a recent project on PDEs, I replicated this state-of-the-art paper's performance with a 3.18x speed increase.

I've also competed in some incredible contests at my university. In the Spring of 2022, I competed in Aggies Invent for First Responders, a 48-hour design competition. My team made PerilScope, an intelligent monitoring system for police vehicles, and we placed 1st out of 14 teams. In the Fall of 2023, I competed in TAMUHack, one of the largest hackathons in Texas. My team made Flock, an app to help travelers make the most of their layovers, and we placed 3rd out of 32 teams in the American Airlines Challenge.

In the summer of 2022, I interned at The Home Depot in the payments division. I built a brand-new microservice to process check payments across all stores, replacing an outdated service with one that was more robust, more secure, easier to maintain, and easier to detect and solve problems. I can't wait to apply my attention to detail and creativity to new domains. If you're interested in getting in contact, connect with me on LinkedIn!
